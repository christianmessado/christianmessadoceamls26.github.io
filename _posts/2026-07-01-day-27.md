what_i_learned: |
Today shifted our focus from coding to technical writing as we continued developing our research paper. We spent time strengthening the Introduction by expanding on the motivation behind post-disaster building damage assessment and emphasizing why rapid infrastructure damage evaluation is becoming increasingly important. I learned that writing a research paper is much more than simply explaining what we did—it requires providing enough background for readers to understand the problem, clearly identifying existing gaps in the literature, and justifying why our research matters.

We also worked on improving the overall organization of the paper using LaTeX and Overleaf. As the paper continues to grow, understanding how sections, citations, and formatting work together has become just as important as understanding the machine learning pipeline itself. It was interesting to see how much attention is given to the structure and readability of a research paper, ensuring that each section flows naturally into the next.

Another major task was developing a comprehensive Limitations section. Rather than viewing limitations as weaknesses, I learned that they demonstrate transparency and help explain the boundaries of our research. We discussed limitations involving dataset imbalance, satellite imagery quality, computational resource constraints, model generalization, and deployment considerations. Reflecting on these challenges helped me better appreciate the practical difficulties of applying artificial intelligence to real-world disaster response.

blockers: |
One of the biggest challenges today was balancing technical accuracy with readability while writing the paper. Explaining complex machine learning concepts in a way that remains clear without oversimplifying them required multiple revisions. We also spent time reorganizing sections to improve the overall flow of the paper and ensure each idea connected naturally to the next.

reflection: |
Today reminded me that research is about much more than building models and writing code. Being able to communicate our work effectively is just as important as developing the technology itself. Every revision helps transform months of research, coding, and experimentation into a professional paper that others can understand and build upon. It's exciting to see our project steadily coming together, and I'm looking forward to continuing to refine the paper as our research progresses.
