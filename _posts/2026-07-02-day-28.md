what_i_learned: |
  Today was a slower-paced day compared to some of our previous weeks, but it was still an important step in moving our project forward. Our team continued working on our research paper in Overleaf, refining sections of the manuscript, improving the overall organization, and ensuring that our methodology, literature review, and project details are clearly presented. Writing a research paper requires much more than simply documenting results—it also involves communicating technical concepts in a way that is logical, concise, and easy for readers to understand.

  In addition to working on the paper, we began reviewing the FastAPI guide that our graduate mentor shared with us. Since one of the final stages of our project involves deploying our damage assessment model, understanding FastAPI will be essential. We spent time familiarizing ourselves with the framework, learning how it can be used to create APIs that serve machine learning models efficiently, and gaining a better understanding of how our trained model will eventually be integrated into a deployable application.

  Although we did not make major coding breakthroughs today, I was reminded that research is not always about producing new code every day. Sometimes the most valuable progress comes from strengthening documentation, planning ahead, and building a deeper understanding of the tools we will use in later stages of the project.
