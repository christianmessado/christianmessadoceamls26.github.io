what_i_learned: |
  Today was our Mid-Summer Symposium, where our team had the opportunity to present the progress we've made on our research project     over the past several weeks. It was exciting to share our work with faculty members, mentors, fellow researchers, and guests while    explaining the goals of our project, the methodology we have developed, and the progress we've made toward building an AI system      for post-disaster building damage assessment.

  Preparing for today's presentation required us to take a large amount of technical information—including our literature review,       dataset preparation, methodology pipeline, model development, and preliminary results—and communicate it in a way that was both       engaging and easy to understand. Throughout the presentation, we worked together to explain complex concepts such as satellite        imagery analysis, U-Net architectures, Vision Transformers, and our training pipeline without overwhelming the audience with          technical details.

  Overall, I think our team did a solid job presenting. We were able to condense weeks of research into a presentation that             effectively highlighted our project's objectives, current accomplishments, and future direction. One of the most rewarding parts of   the symposium was interacting with attendees after our presentation. We answered several thoughtful questions about our               methodology, research goals, and the technologies we're using, which gave us an opportunity to demonstrate our understanding of the   project and reflect on how much we've learned since the internship began.

blockers: |
  Our biggest challenge today was managing our time while presenting. Because each slide contained a significant amount of important    information, it was sometimes difficult to balance how much each team member spoke before transitioning to the next slide.            Although we stayed within the overall presentation, we recognized that improving the pacing and distribution of speaking time         would make future presentations even smoother and more polished.

reflection: |
  Presenting at the Mid-Summer Symposium was a great milestone in our internship. Looking back at where we started just a few weeks     ago, it's amazing to see how much our team has accomplished. Not only have we gained technical knowledge in artificial intelligence   and computer vision, but we've also become more confident communicators. Today's presentation showed that we can effectively          explain complex research to a broad audience while working together as a team. Although there's always room for improvement,          especially with time management during presentations, I'm proud of how we represented our research and look forward to continuing     to build on this momentum during the second half of the internship.
