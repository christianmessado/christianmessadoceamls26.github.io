what_i_learned: |
  Today was focused almost entirely on research, and it was one of the most productive days we've had so far. My team continued reviewing several research papers related to change detection and post-disaster building damage assessment. While these papers contain a lot of technical information, they have helped us better understand the strengths and limitations of existing approaches.

  I spent time analyzing both the Daudt et al. (2018) and Chen et al. (2020) papers. One of the key things I learned was how Siamese networks can be used to compare pre-disaster and post-disaster satellite imagery, as well as how attention mechanisms can improve a model's ability to identify meaningful changes while ignoring noise. We also worked on refining our methodology and outlining the steps our project will take from data collection all the way to model evaluation.

blockers: |
  The biggest challenge today was the sheer amount of information contained within the research papers. Understanding the architecture details, evaluation metrics, and experimental results takes time, especially when trying to connect each paper back to our own project. However, the challenge is manageable, and each paper review is helping us build a stronger foundation for our research.

reflection: |
  Although reading and analyzing research papers can be demanding, I can definitely see how much progress our team is making. Every paper we review gives us new ideas and helps us better understand the decisions we need to make for our own project. It is also rewarding to see our literature review, methodology, and project direction becoming more organized and refined.

  Overall, today reminded me that research is not just about finding answers—it's about learning how to ask better questions. As we continue moving forward, I am excited to transition from studying existing work to eventually building and testing our own models.
