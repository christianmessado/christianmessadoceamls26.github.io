what_i_learned: |
  Today was dedicated to preparing for our upcoming Mid-Summer Symposium. As a team, we spent the day reviewing our presentation slides, refining our speaking points, and making sure we could clearly communicate the progress we've made throughout our research project. Since our audience would include faculty members, mentors, fellow researchers, and guests, we focused on presenting technical information in a way that would be understandable to both technical and non-technical audiences.

  We carefully reviewed each section of our presentation, including our project motivation, literature review, methodology pipeline, dataset preparation, and model development. One thing I learned today is that giving an effective presentation involves much more than simply reading from slides. It's important to understand the material well enough to explain it naturally, maintain audience engagement, and smoothly transition between topics.

  Throughout the day, we practiced multiple times as a group, making adjustments to our explanations and ensuring each team member was comfortable with their assigned sections. We also worked on anticipating potential questions from the audience so we would be prepared to confidently discuss our research and explain the reasoning behind our methodology and technical decisions.

blockers: |
  Our primary challenge today was balancing the amount of information on each slide with the limited presentation time. Because our project covers many technical topics, it was difficult deciding what details to include and what information could be summarized. We also worked on improving the flow between speakers to create a more seamless presentation.

reflection: |
  Today's preparation reminded me that communication is just as important as technical knowledge in research. Even the best research has limited impact if it cannot be explained clearly to others. Practicing our presentation helped increase my confidence and reinforced how much our team has accomplished over the past several weeks. I'm looking forward to presenting our work at the Mid-Summer Symposium and sharing everything we've learned throughout this internship.
