what_i_learned: |
  Today our team had the opportunity to present our Final Symposium presentation to the program directors and coordinators. It was a great experience because it gave us the chance to showcase everything we've been working on throughout the summer and receive valuable feedback before the official symposium.

  Overall, our presentation went really well. Everyone on the team did an excellent job explaining their respective sections, and our hard work over the past several weeks was reflected in the quality of the presentation. One of the biggest highlights was our demo video, which received very positive feedback from the program directors. They really enjoyed it, and it was rewarding to see our creative approach resonate with them.

  I learned that confidence, preparation, and teamwork are just as important as having strong technical content. Presenting to an audience before the symposium gave us a better idea of what we do well and what we can continue improving before the final presentation.

Since we are the first group presenting at the Final Symposium this weekend, we want to make sure we set a high standard. Our biggest challenge now is continuing to practice so that each team member presents with confidence, energy, and smooth transitions between speakers.

  Another area we want to improve is making our delivery feel even more natural so that we can fully engage the audience while clearly explaining our research.

I'm excited for the symposium and proud of everything our team has accomplished throughout this internship.
