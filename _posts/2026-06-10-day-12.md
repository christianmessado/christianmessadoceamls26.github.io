what_i_learned: |
Today was focused on refining the methodology for our post-disaster building damage assessment project. As our literature review continues, we have started translating what we've learned from previous research into a structured workflow for our own study. This process helped me better understand how the different components of a computer vision project fit together, from data preparation all the way to deployment.

One of the main tasks was developing a methodology pipeline that outlines each stage of our project. The pipeline begins with dataset acquisition and GeoTIFF preprocessing, followed by building patch extraction, class imbalance analysis, data augmentation, PyTorch data loading, model training, evaluation, and deployment. Breaking the project into these stages made it easier to visualize the complete research process and understand how each step contributes to the final goal.

blockers: |
No major blockers today. The biggest challenge was organizing the workflow in a way that accurately represents the project's methodology while remaining clear and easy to communicate in our presentation materials.

reflection: |
Today's work reminded me that successful machine learning projects require much more than simply training a model. A significant amount of effort goes into data preparation, preprocessing, experimentation, evaluation, and deployment planning. Creating the methodology pipeline helped me appreciate the complexity of the research process and gave me a clearer understanding of how our project will progress moving forward. I'm excited to continue building upon this foundation as we transition from research and planning into implementation.
