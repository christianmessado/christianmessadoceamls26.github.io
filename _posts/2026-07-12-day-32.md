what_i_learned: |
  Today our team focused on finding the best template for our poster presentation. We explored the CBEIS building and looked at several poster presentations that were displayed throughout the building to get ideas for our own design. Seeing different layouts, color schemes, and ways researchers organized their work gave us inspiration and helped us understand what makes a poster both visually appealing and easy to follow.

  After gathering ideas, we began building our own poster presentation using figures and images from our weekly project PowerPoint presentations. Reusing the visuals we created throughout the internship gave us a solid starting point and helped us begin organizing the different sections of our research into a cohesive poster.

  By the end of the day, we had a much better understanding of how we wanted our poster to look. We now have a clear direction for the design and simply need to continue building on that foundation.

blockers: |
  One challenge today was deciding which poster layout would best showcase our research. There were many great examples throughout the building, so choosing a design that balanced visual appeal with clear organization took careful thought.

  Another challenge was determining how to arrange our figures and content so that the poster would effectively communicate our methodology, results, and conclusions without becoming overcrowded.

reflection: |
  Today showed me how important presentation and design are when sharing research. A well-designed poster can make complex technical information much easier to understand and allows viewers to quickly follow the story of the project.

  Exploring existing posters gave us valuable inspiration, and I'm excited to continue developing our own presentation. We have a strong foundation in place, and now it's about building on that idea to create a professional poster that effectively showcases all of the work we've accomplished throughout the internship.
