what_i_learned: |
  Today we finished our elevator pitch video for the CEAMLS Summer Research Internship. It was a really exciting experience being able to come together as a group and work on a project that allowed us to showcase our research in a creative way. I learned that communicating research is not only about the technical details, but also about being able to explain the purpose, impact, and importance of the project in a way that others can understand.

  Working on the video pitch helped me improve my ability to present our research story and highlight the motivation behind our AI-powered post-disaster building damage assessment project. It was rewarding to see all of our ideas come together into a final product.

  In addition to completing the elevator pitch video, we also continued making progress on our poster presentation. We worked on adding important sections such as our methodology and other details that would help explain our research process. This allowed us to think about how we could visually communicate our pipeline, approach, and results in a clear and organized way.

blockers: |
  One challenge was making sure everyone’s ideas and contributions were represented while keeping the video concise and engaging. Since we had a limited amount of time to explain our project, we had to carefully choose the most important information to include.

  For the poster presentation, one challenge was deciding how to organize the information so that the research process could be easily understood by someone viewing the poster for the first time. We wanted to make sure that the methodology and technical components were explained clearly without overwhelming the audience.

reflection: |
  Today was a really memorable part of the program because it gave us an opportunity to step away from the technical side of research and focus on storytelling. I enjoyed collaborating with my team and seeing everyone contribute their own ideas to the final video. I’m excited to see everyone else's elevator pitch videos and learn how other teams chose to present their projects.

  Continuing to work on the poster presentation also reminded me how important it is to communicate research effectively. After spending weeks building our project, preparing datasets, and developing our AI pipeline, being able to summarize our work visually is an important final step. These experiences have helped me become more comfortable presenting technical information to different audiences.
