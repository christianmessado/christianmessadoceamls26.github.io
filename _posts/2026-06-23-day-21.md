what_i_learned: |
  Today was a significant milestone for our research project because we finally got our Step 6 model training pipeline running successfully after spending considerable time troubleshooting errors and configuration issues. Over the past several days, we encountered a variety of challenges related to file paths, dataset loading, imports, and training setup. Through this process, I learned how important debugging is in machine learning projects and how even small mistakes can prevent an entire training pipeline from running correctly.

  Once the issues were resolved, we were able to start training our baseline U-Net model. The training process is expected to take a long time because each completed progress bar represents a successful training epoch, and there are many epochs remaining before training is finished. Even though the process is slow, it was exciting to finally see the model running and producing results after all of the preparation work that went into building the pipeline.

  In addition to model training, we also began making progress on our Mid-Symposium presentation. We started organizing our slides and identifying the key accomplishments, research findings, and technical contributions that we want to share. Preparing for the presentation helped me reflect on how much work our team has completed since the beginning of the program and how each phase of the project connects to the overall research objective.
