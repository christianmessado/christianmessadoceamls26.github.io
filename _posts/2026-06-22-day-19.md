what_i_learned: |
Today was focused on both research documentation and continued progress on our model training efforts. One of the main topics covered was Overleaf, an online LaTeX platform commonly used for writing academic papers and research publications. I learned how Overleaf helps research teams collaborate on technical documents, manage citations, and organize sections such as the introduction, methodology, results, and conclusions.

We also discussed how our research paper will be structured and how the work we have completed so far—including literature reviews, dataset preparation, model development, and model training—will contribute to different sections of the final manuscript. Learning about the publication process helped me better understand how research findings are communicated to the broader scientific community.

In addition to research writing, we continued monitoring the progress of our baseline model training pipeline. As the model trains, we are gaining experience interpreting outputs, tracking experiments through Weights & Biases, and preparing for future comparisons between different architectures and training strategies.

Another important part of today's activities involved learning how to properly complete internship timesheets and maintain accurate records of our work. We reviewed the importance of documenting hours worked, recording accomplishments, and submitting timesheets correctly. This helped reinforce the professional and administrative responsibilities that accompany technical research work.

blockers: |
No major blockers occurred today. Most of the day's work was focused on understanding research documentation, learning how to use Overleaf effectively, reviewing timesheet procedures, and monitoring the progress of model training experiments.

reflection: |
Today's experience showed me that successful research involves much more than coding and experimentation. Writing technical papers, documenting progress, maintaining accurate records, and communicating results are all critical components of the research process. Learning how to use Overleaf and understanding the structure of an academic paper gave me a greater appreciation for the work that goes into publishing research. I also gained a better understanding of the professional responsibilities associated with internships and research projects, which will be valuable throughout my academic and professional career.
