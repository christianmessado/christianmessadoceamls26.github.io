what_i_learned: |
  Today was focused on preparing our dataset and workflow for the next phase of the project: model training. Building on the work completed with the xBD/xView2 dataset, we continued refining the data preparation process to ensure the training pipeline would have properly organized and formatted inputs.

  One of the main tasks involved finalizing the creation of image pairs from the pre-disaster and post-disaster satellite imagery. Since our project aims to assess building damage following natural disasters, correctly matching corresponding images is critical for enabling accurate analysis and model development. Working through this process helped me better understand how change detection and damage assessment datasets are structured.

  We also continued organizing building patches that will be used during model training. Breaking large satellite images into smaller patches makes the data more manageable and allows deep learning models to focus on individual building regions. Through this process, I gained a better understanding of how raw satellite imagery is transformed into training-ready datasets.

  In addition, our team reviewed the methodology pipeline developed for the project. Revisiting each stage of the workflow—from dataset acquisition and preprocessing to training, evaluation, and deployment—helped reinforce how the different components fit together. By the end of the day, we were well-positioned to transition into Step 6, which focuses on model training and experimentation.

blockers: |
  No major blockers occurred today. The primary challenge was ensuring that image pairs, building patches, and dataset organization were completed correctly before moving into model development. Careful verification was necessary to avoid issues later during training.

reflection: |
  Today's work highlighted the importance of data preparation in machine learning projects. While model architectures often receive the most attention, the quality and organization of the dataset play an equally important role in achieving strong results. Finalizing image pairs, organizing training patches, and reviewing the methodology pipeline helped establish a solid foundation for the model training phase. I am excited to begin implementing and evaluating deep learning models as our project continues to progress.
