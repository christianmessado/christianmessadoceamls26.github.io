what_i_learned: |
  Today our research cohort traveled to Washington, D.C. to attend the AWS Summit, where we had the opportunity to learn about the      latest innovations in cloud computing, artificial intelligence, machine learning, cybersecurity, and data analytics. It was an        incredible experience to hear directly from industry professionals about how Amazon Web Services is helping organizations build       scalable, secure, and intelligent applications across a wide variety of fields.

  Throughout the day, I attended several sessions and explored the exhibition floor, where companies demonstrated real-world            applications of AWS technologies. One of the biggest takeaways was seeing how cloud infrastructure enables AI systems to process      massive amounts of data efficiently while supporting collaboration, model deployment, and large-scale machine learning workflows.     Many of the presentations emphasized the growing importance of generative AI, responsible AI development, and cloud-native            solutions for solving complex problems.

  The summit also reinforced many of the concepts we've been learning throughout our summer research project. As we continue            developing our post-disaster building damage assessment system, it became clear how cloud platforms like AWS can support every        stage of the machine learning lifecycle—from storing large satellite imagery datasets to training models with powerful computing      resources and deploying applications for end users. Seeing these technologies applied in industry helped bridge the gap between our   academic research and real-world implementation.

  Beyond the technical sessions, I enjoyed networking with professionals, students, and technology companies from across the country.   Learning about different career paths, internships, certifications, and emerging technologies gave me a broader perspective on        opportunities within computer science and reinforced my excitement about pursuing a career in technology after graduation.

blockers: |
  There were no major blockers today since the day was dedicated to professional development and learning. The biggest challenge was    deciding which sessions to attend because there were so many interesting topics happening throughout the summit. Although it wasn't   possible to attend every presentation, I was able to gain valuable insights from the sessions I participated in and from              conversations with industry professionals.

reflection: |
  Attending the AWS Summit was one of the highlights of the internship so far. It was inspiring to be surrounded by thousands of        professionals who are shaping the future of cloud computing and artificial intelligence. The experience reminded me that the skills   we're developing through our research project have direct applications in industry and that continuous learning is essential in the   rapidly evolving field of technology. I left the summit feeling motivated to continue strengthening my technical skills, exploring    cloud technologies, and applying what I've learned to both my research and future career opportunities.
