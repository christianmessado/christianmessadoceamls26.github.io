what_i_learned: |
  Today was centered on refining the communication side of our research project by continuing work on both our research poster and our Overleaf paper. While much of our previous work focused on developing the methodology, training the baseline model, and reviewing literature, today emphasized presenting that work in a clear and professional manner.

  We continued organizing the poster by refining sections such as the methodology, materials and methods, objectives, workflow pipeline, and literature review. This process taught me that creating an effective research poster involves much more than placing text on a page—it requires presenting complex technical information in a way that is concise, visually engaging, and easy for others to understand. Every figure, flowchart, and section must contribute to telling the story of the research.

  We also made additional progress on our Overleaf paper by improving the organization of the content, reviewing previous sections, and ensuring that our methodology and related work accurately reflect the progress we have made throughout the project. As we revised the paper, I gained a better appreciation for the importance of technical writing and how research papers communicate not only the results but also the reasoning and methodology behind the work.

blockers: |
  One challenge today was deciding how to condense detailed technical information into a poster format without losing important context. Since posters have limited space, we had to carefully prioritize the most significant findings, visuals, and explanations while maintaining clarity. We also spent time refining wording throughout the paper to improve readability and consistency.

reflection: |
  Today's work reminded me that research is not only about developing models and analyzing data—it is also about effectively communicating the work to others. Preparing the poster and refining our paper helped reinforce everything we have accomplished over the past several weeks, from dataset preparation and model development to literature reviews and methodology design. Although polishing documentation can be time-consuming, it is an essential part of the research process and will help us present our work confidently at the symposium. With each revision, our project becomes more organized, professional, and ready for presentation.
