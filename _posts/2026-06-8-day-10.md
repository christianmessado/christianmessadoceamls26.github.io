what_i_learned: |
  Today was dedicated entirely to research work with our graduate mentor, and it felt great to spend the whole day focused on our project. As we continue working together, it's becoming clear how much progress our team has made, both in terms of the research itself and how effectively we collaborate. Every day, we seem to become more organized, confident, and productive as a group.

  A large portion of the day was spent reviewing and discussing research papers related to post-disaster building damage assessment and computer vision. Through these discussions, I gained a deeper understanding of how researchers use satellite imagery and deep learning models to identify and classify building damage after natural disasters. I also learned more about the importance of literature reviews in the research process. Rather than simply reading papers, we analyzed their methodologies, compared their approaches, and identified limitations that could help shape our own research direction.

  Another important lesson from today was understanding how different computer vision architectures approach change detection and damage assessment tasks. As we reviewed previous studies, I began to see how model design choices, dataset selection, and evaluation metrics can significantly impact research outcomes. These discussions helped me better understand why establishing a strong research foundation is critical before moving into model development and experimentation.

  We also continued refining our understanding of the overall project workflow, including dataset preparation, model training, evaluation, and deployment. Seeing how all of these components connect helped me develop a clearer picture of the research process as a whole. While there is still a lot to learn, today's work reinforced the importance of careful planning and thorough research before implementing technical solutions.
